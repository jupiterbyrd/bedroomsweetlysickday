BEDROOM sweetly sick day

Hi Quiet Life Co Team!


This song explores Byung-Chul Han's concept of the achievement-subject from his book, The Burnout Society. The speaker is a citizen of the 21st century, kitted out with inmmensely powerful technology that's become unbiquitous. Most importantly, they have the freedom to be who they want to be.


Full of reverb and looming with all the sinister spirit of witchy chants in the night, the speaker is haunted by The Four Horsemen: Serotonin, Oxytocin, Endorphins and Dopamine meant to bring Eden on Earth. I mean they are the feel-good chemicals so what is Eden if not constant, never-ending good feelings? Right? More so in a world where the sounds of ukuleles are so processed they sound like gritty guitars. It's clear the speaker feels like a god!

But it's all wrong. Physically, the speaker knows it. They are enundated by grotesque symptoms. The oversaturation of feeling is "tickling flesh and tissues" and "pickling blood and body". This is a piece of body horror. It's clear in the synthy bell that carries a sweetness that grows ironic over the course of the song. This is about a sickness, an illness of "too-much-sameness" as Byung-Chul Han calls it.


The song is produced by me in the spirit of pushing the limits of what manipulations I can do to acoustic sounds until it's unbearable. My approach is to be maximalistic, almost overwhelming. There is almost too much percussion. The vocals are languishing: they trail on longer than they need to.


Culminating in the lorem ipsum section, the sings follows the progression from simple indulgence to overstimulation to the dissolution of meaning. Lorem ipsum dolor. It's placeholder used when doing graphic design. In the same way that lorem ipsum text is used to give the impression of how copy would look in a design, a growing portion of what we experience, SEO-optimised articles, AI slop, the works, do just that: they approximates meaning. You have to squint, really hard, to see it as anything more than aggregational gobbledygook because what it is lacks true substance. This is Dead Internet Theory. This is a human existence becoming less organic.

The opening line of the chorus interpolates Where Is My Mind? by the Pixies from their 1988 debut album Surfer Rosa. BEDROOM sweetly sick day borrows the absurdity and in that final chorus, there is a melding of scenarios where the speaker in this "remembers" diving like the speaker in Where Is My Mind? did in the Caribbean.

Ultimately, this song is an exploration of our current state of overstimulation and adds to the canon of images that accompany Byung-Chul Han's concept of the achievement-subject ✦

{ About the Artist }

Jupiterbyrd (she/her) concerns herself with world-building; everything is in service to the concept and each word is shouted in the language of pop. She participates in the intricacy of real things. Juxtaposed against iridescent instrumentals, her themes are grounded in contemporary realities. She captures anxiety lived collectively. She captures youth lived digitally. She captures moments understood instinctually. With a background in poetry, she finds comfort in layering meaning on top of meaning, yielding lyrics that are vivid, observational and honest.

Sonically, she speaks a dialect of pop that's louder, crunchier and almost uncomfortably vulnerable. Jupiterbyrd cherishes being in the tradition of electronica and her music resonates with the style of artists like Dorian Electra and Jane Remover. She employs the high-concept artfulness of Caroline Polachek and FKA Twigs, the melodic prowess of Shygirl and James Mercer of The Shins, and she melds it with the emotional transparency of Pierce The Veil and Issy Wood. Jupiterbyrd takes a Regina Spektoresque poetic voice and morphs it into her particular portrayal of queer black womanhood.

Otherworldly and superlative: as her name suggests, Jupiterbyrd conjures worlds, charting constellations glowing against reality's night sky ✦

I am planning my first solo live show, BLUEPRINTS. This show emerged out of my desire to capture the structures around the artistic space I'm in. I'm very early in my career; I am at my most uninformed. Yet, here I am building the foundation of my musical and artistic identity, choosing how to present myself, what niche I hope to occupy, what my sound will be and even choosing my name.

In a landscape where the internet is forever and you are as much your brand as you are you, this show hopes to explore all of the emotions I feel regarding this. This show will include performances of my singles, some original spoken word pieces, covers and the show's titular song, BLUEPRINTS, that will be released the morning of the show.

This will be my first solo live show and it's in the very early stages of production. It's being produced by my sister and I. What is particularly exciting about this show is that, to end it off, I will be announcing the title and release day of my debut album: RUMI'S HOUSE OF MINE.


It's is a project that was born out of my reading of The Guesthouse by Sufi poet and scholar Rumi. This album imagines Jupiterbyrd as 'Father Jupiterbyrd', custodian of my human, my guesthouse and chronicles what each room contains, what happens within them, who is there and in so doing depicts key parts of my emotion landscape, internal space in an increasingly precarious world. It explores themes of depression, connection & alienation, and, naturally, interiority: what does it feel like to be me.


I would love to discuss all thus in person. So much of the project is written and produced and I'd eat up an opportunity to wax poetic about it ✦
